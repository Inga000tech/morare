Kintsugi

The beauty of the break.

↓
History

15th Century Japan

Shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu sent a damaged tea bowl back to China for repairs. It returned held together with ugly metal staples. Japanese craftsmen found a better way: filling the cracks with gold.

Deepen your curiosity

This birthed a philosophy where repair is not something to hide, but a testament to the object's history and resilience. The "scar" becomes the most valuable part.

The Word
"The world breaks everyone and afterward many are strong at the broken places."

— Ernest Hemingway

The Ritual

Close your eyes. Identify one place in your body holding tension. Breathe into it, then exhale it out.

What is a "crack" in your life that you are learning to fill with gold?
